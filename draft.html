Illegal Deportation on
Long Island?

By: Joshua Cohen

On November 20th, lawyers from the Central American Refugee Center(CARCEN), a Long Island based immigration services center, and the Hofstra Law Clinic filed suit against Nassau County in New York Supreme Court for illegal practices in detaining immigrants.
They claim the Nassau County Police Department has violated New York State law by detaining people on warrants issued by the U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement Agency...
even without authorities having any proof
that the person has commited a crime.
One of the victims of the illegal Nassau County detains was 30-year old Hempstead resident, Denis Guerra Guerra.

Image Credit: Long Island Wins

During a routine traffic stop in August for faling to signal when changing lanes, Denis Guerra Guerra was arrested and later deported to El Salvador when it was discovered he had a 10-year old pending order of removal from ICE."


Mr.Guerra's deportation sparked a major protest in the Long Island community.

Of the 12 million undocumeted immigrants living in the United States, Long Island is home to an estimated 125,000 immigrants who contribute close to $40 billion to the NY economy according to a study by Newsday in March.

Long Island is also a region that has seen a 31 percent increase in arrests of immigrants sought for deportation under the Trump Administration according to data from ICE.
